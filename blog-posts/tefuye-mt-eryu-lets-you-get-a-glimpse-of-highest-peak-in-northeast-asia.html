Tefuye Old Trail and Mt. Eryu: A Trail That Lets You Get A Glimpse of Highest Peak in Northeast Asia

Posted by:  Anusha Lee
Disclaimer: Please note some of the following are based on my personal condition, and I will try to provide the latest and correct information as possible as I can. Please feel free to let me know if I make any mistake and thank you for reading. Please also note that your safety is your own responsibility.

How to Get There

Zhizhong locates in Nantou County, but very close to Jiayi County where Mt. Ali locates. There’s only one bus to get here, Yuanlin Bus 6739. Bus 6739 runs between Sun Moon Lake and Mt. Ali. If you plan to visit Sun Moon Lake and have some extra days, it will be great to put Tefuye and Mt. Ali in your itinerary.
After getting off at Zhizhong stop, you will find the trailhead of Tefuye and a public toilet. Some people camp near the police station. It’s better to check with them first if you can camp there.

Peaks to Reach

Mt. Zhizhong, or Mt. Eryu, 自忠山 (兒玉山) Zhizhong Shan or Eryu Shan, 2,606m (8,550 ft). Views are very good on the summit.
Mt. Beixia 北霞山, Beixia Shan, 2,472 m (8,110 ft). No views.
Mt. Dongsui 東水山, Dongsui Shan, 2,611 m (8,566 ft). No views.

Take the Challenging Part to See the Highest Peak in Northeast Asia

Tefuye trail locates in Alishan National Scenic Area Administration in south Taiwan, and Mt. Ali or Alisha, is one of the most beautiful attractions in Taiwan. If you arrange this hike, you probably head south to Chiayi from Taipei and spend one night around here like us.
After getting off our hired bus in Zhizhong 自忠 around 2,288 m/ 7,507 ft, we were amazed by the views between in the valleys. Our guide told us this is a great spot to see the beautiful sunrise and you can camp nearby. If the clear sky and the mountains we witnessed at that moment was already astonishing, I can’t imagine how stunning it will be if I get a chance to see the sunrise.
The entrance of Tefuye trail is very obvious from Zhizhong side and there is an old railway, and you just follow the railway to get inside. There are many cedar trees and the trail is very flat. After a few meters, our happy time was over, because we took this trail head inside the trail to start our journey. To demonstrate its authority, the trail gave us a very steep slope.
A little warning before you decide to take this trail. This trail is considered as a high altitude one, and the high altitude will make the breathing a little bit more difficult than hiking at the lower ones.
Our group left Taipei late, and by the time we got to our B&B, it was 1 am. We had to get up at 5 am, which means we didn’t have enough sleep. When we were about to leave our B&B, we were amazed by the surroundings.
I wasn’t feeling well on that week. Moreover, the trail to our first summit was very steep. So, those things together made my hike here much more difficult than I thought. Therefore, if you plan to hike here, try to get enough sleep and be aware of the high altitude.
Our first summit was Mt. Zhizhong or Mt. Eryu, 2,606 m/ 8,550 ft. The trail condition was quite good, some parts were covered by pine needles, and some rocks, but it was still very steep. We reached a place that was big enough for all of us to take a rest, and left our backpacks here before climbing up to the summit.
I thought the previous trail was steep enough, it was even steeper here. It didn’t take us long to get to Mt. Zhizhong.
I looked the other side from the summit and our guide told us that that was the highest mountain in Taiwan, Mt. Yu, 3,952 m/ 12,966 ft.
The view from the opposite side of Mt. Yu was also spectacular. I stood there for a while to admire the landscape around me.
We still had several summits to go. Our next stop is Mt. Dongshui. The trail became steeper, and I also felt more tired. I also noticed the higher altitude we hiked, the more amazing trees we saw. Our guded reminded us that logging was the main business in Tefuye.
I’m no expert of vegetation, but you still can tell the woods is mixed with pines and subtropical trees.
While hiking on the trail and enjoy the beautiful trees, I noticed some big trees were brutally chopped down with signs. I went up to those trees and the signs said that those trees were the victims of illegal logging, and the police have started the investigations. This wasn't my first time seeing this. Illegal logging is still an issue in Taiwan. The government promises they will release the regulations of hiking in Taiwan and encourage hikers to report poachers if seeing them. Hopefully this will help.
I also noticed there were many acorns on the trail. Our guide told us those were acorns of Ring-cupped Oak trees 青剛櫟. Those seeds are one of Formosan Black Bears' favorite food. But you don't need to worry that you will face to face with those bears. Formosan Black Bears are endangered species in Taiwan. It's very difficult to see them on the trails. Seeing those reminds me of Scrat in the movie of Ice Age.
Mt. Dongshui 東水山, 2,611 m / 8,566 ft wasn't far away from Mt. Zhizhong, about one hour, but there was no view here, so we proceed to our next summit, Mt. Beixia, 北霞山.
Mt. Beixia, 北霞山, 2,472 m/ 8,110 ft, was much lower than Mt. Dongshui, and the round trip took around 4 hours, at least for me. This section wasn't an exciting hike, and my pace was getting slower and slower, especially during the ascending.
Before my first visit of Mt. Dongshui, I already found myself huffing and puffing, and I had to stop almost every few meters to catch my breath. A very friendly guide, Fion, decided to keep company with me. I even asked Fion whether there was an early exit on the trail. Unfortunately, no.
My pace was getting slower and slower, and during the most difficult time I had to stop every three steps to take a rest. I did difficult hikes before, but i never experienced things like this before. I got disoriented sometimes that I missed the trail. So, Fion decided to take the lead.
Fion was in a much better condition compared to mine, and I felt so terrible for her that she had to get slow down for me. I remember I fell asleep within seconds during a break when I leaned on my backpack. When I woke up, I looked into the woods and was amazed by the beautiful lighting and the woods, and I took this photo.
By the time we left the rugged trail, it was already dark. But we reached the flat trail, which was the easy part of Tefuye Trail. It was pity that I didn’t get to see this part of the trail in the broad daylight, but it didn’t matter at that moment. I borrowed some photos to show how beautiful the trail was.
Both Fion and I just wanted to get back to our bus as soon as we could. We took our headlamps and continued to hike. It started drizzling, too, and I could see the droplets through my headlamp.
It was around 2 km / 1.2 miles to get to our starting point on that morning and took me more than one hour to finish, but it felt like forever. Although I was very slow, I wasn’t the last one to finish our first-day hike. There were around 7 people behind Fion and I, and it took another two hours for them to finish the hike. For me, I was glad that I could call it a day.

The Easier Part of Tefuye Trail

I only took around a few meters on the easy part of the trail during the board daylight, but I really want to know how it feels like to see all those trees. For those who just want to enjoy the roaming without stress, you can find some information at Tefuye Historical Trail from Forestry Bureau website.

Some Thoughts about How to Make This Hike More Enjoyable

I will do this hike again if I get a chance, but I will change some of the things to make the hike more enjoyable.
First, I will leave Taipei earlier. We left Taipei around 8 pm and we arrived at our B&B around 1 am, which took us around 6 hours to Mt. Ali area.
Second, I will camp near Zhizhong Police Station to reduce the commuting time. It took us almost 2 hours from our B&B to Tefuye Trail. If we could camp near the police station, we could have slept more and even seen the sunrise. You will find a public toilet and water nearby. Why our lead guide arranged B&B for us was to hope we could sleep better, but we could only sleep around 3 hours, which didn’t help much.
Third, I probably will skip Mt. Beixia, make it a point-to-point route, and take the same route back to where we stared, and spend some time to enjoy part of the railway trail.
Fourth, during our hike, I saw many maple leaves on the trail, and it was still very beautiful. If you want to see fall foliage, I think you can arrange the hike when the timing is right.
I still highly recommend this trail, as long you can avoid my mistakes, or just take the easy one and enjoy the unique landscapes and the history behind the trail. After this, you can also visit Mt. Ali and see more spectacular views over there.
