Tefuye Old Trail and Mt. Eryu: A Trail That Lets You Get A Glimpse of Highest Peak in Northeast Asia

Posted by:  Anusha Lee
Disclaimer: Please note some of the following are based on my personal condition, and I will try to provide the latest and correct information as possible as I can. Please feel free to let me know if I make any mistake and thank you for reading. Please also note that your safety is your own responsibility.

Take the Challenging Part to See the Highest Peak in Northeast Asia

Tefuye trail locates in Alishan National Scenic Area Administration in south Taiwan, and Mt. Ali or Alisha, is one of the most beautiful attractions in Taiwan. If you arrange this hike, you probably head south to Chiayi from Taipei and spend one night around here like us.
After getting off our hired bus in Zhizhong 自忠 around 2,288 m/ 7,507 ft, we were amazed by the views between in the valleys. Our guide told us this is a great spot to see the beautiful sunrise and you can camp nearby. If the haze and the mountains we witnessed at that moment was already astonishing, I can’t imagine how stunning it will be if I get a chance to see the sunrise.
The entrance of Tefuye trail is very obvious from Zhizhong side and there is an old railway, and you just follow the railway to get inside. There are many cedar trees and the trail is very flat. After a few meters, our happy time was over, because we took this trail head inside the trail to start our journey. To demonstrate its authority, the trail gave us a very steep slope.
A little warning before you decide to take this trail. This trail is considered as a high altitude one, and the high altitude will make the breathing a little bit more difficult than hiking at the lower ones.
Our group left Taipei late, and by the time we got to our B&B, it was 1 am. We had to get up at 5 am, which means we didn’t have enough sleep. I wasn’t feeling well on that week. So, those things together made my hike here much more difficult than I thought. Therefore, if you plan to hike here, try to get enough sleep and be aware of the high altitude.
Our first summit was Mt. Eryu or Mt. Zhizhong, 2,606 m/ 8,550 ft. The trail condition was quite good, some parts were covered by pine needles, and some rocks, but it was still very steep. It didn’t take us long to get to Mt. Zhizhong.
We left our backpacks here and climbed up to the summit. I thought the previous trail was steep enough, it was even more steep here. Luckily, it didn’t take us long to get to the summit. Then, we got our first glimpse of the highest peak in Northeast Asian, Mt. Yu, 3,952 m/ 12,966 ft.
I’m no expert of vegetation, and I think the only thing I can recognize is bamboos, but you still can tell the woods is mixed with pines and subtropical trees.
Our next summit is Mt. Dongshui 東水山, 2,611 m / 8,566 ft. There was no view here, so we proceed to our next summit, Mt. Beixia, 北霞山, 2,472 m/ 8,110 ft.
We made a P like loop, and we got back to Mt. Dongshui twice to take the trail to Mt. Beixia. Before my first visit of Mt. Dongshui, I wasn’t feeling well. It was very difficult for me to breathe, and I had to stop almost every few meters to catch my breath. A very friendly guide, Fion, decided to keep company with me.
My pace was getting slower and slower, and I had to stop every three steps to take a rest. During that time, I was wondering why I was so weak. I took hikes with similar difficult rating before, but this one was so hard. I got disoriented sometimes that I missed the trail. So, Fion decided to take the lead.
Fion was in a much better condition compared to mine, and I felt so terrible for her that she had to get slow down for me. I remember I fell asleep within seconds during a break when I leaned on my backpack. When I woke up, I looked into the woods and was amazed by the beautiful lighting and the woods, and I took this photo.
By the time we left the rugged trail, it was already dark. But we reached the flat trail, which was the easy part of Tefuye Trail. It was pity that I didn’t get to see this part of the trail in the broad daylight, but it didn’t matter at that moment. Both Fion and I just wanted to get back to our bus as soon as we could. We took our headlamps and continued to hike. It started drizzling, too, and I could see the droplets through my headlamp.
It was around 2 km / 1.2 miles to get to our starting point on that morning, but it felt like forever to me. Although I was very slow, I wasn’t the last one to finish our first-day hike. There were around 7 people behind Fion and I.
One thing worth to mention is we saw many acorns of Ring-cupped Oak trees. Those seeds are one of Formosan Black Bears' favorite food. But you don't need to worry that you will face to face with those bears. Formosan Black Bears are endangered species in Taiwan. It's very difficult to see them on the trails. Seeing those reminds me of Scrat in the movie of Ice Age.

Some Thoughts about How to Make This Hike More Enjoyable

I will do this hike again if I get a chance, but I will change some of the things to make the hike more enjoyable.
First, I will leave Taipei earlier. We left Taipei around 8 pm and we arrived at our B&B around 1 am, which took us around 6 hours to Mt. Ali area.
Second, I will camp near Zhizhong Police Station to reduce the commuting time. It took us almost 2 hours from our B&B to Tefuye Trail. If we could camp near the police station, we could have slept more and even seen the sunrise. You will find a public toilet and water nearby. Why our lead guide arranged B&B for us was to hope we could sleep better, but we could only sleep around 3 hours, which didn’t help much.
Third, I probably will skip Mt. Beixia, make it a point-to-point route, and take the same route back to where we stared, and spend some time to enjoy part of the railway trail.
Fourth, during our hike, I saw many maple leaves on the trail, and it was still very beautiful. If you want to see fall foliage, I think you can arrange the hike when the timing is right.
I still highly recommend this trail, as long you can avoid my mistakes, or just take the easy one and enjoy the unique landscapes and the history behind the trail. After this, you can also visit Mt. Ali (link) and see more spectacular views over there.
